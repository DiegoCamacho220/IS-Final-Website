    • How I became a Swimmer

    I became a swimmer when I was around 4 years old, when my mother decided it was needed so I wouldn't drown in case of an emergency. But she didn't know she was showing me one of my biggest passions, and in that very moment, she changed my entire life. When I was around 7 years old I entered the state junior team which involved high performance practices and competitions. My passion for swimming only grew, which motivated me to get better and better. As years passed, I kept achieving my personal goals to the point of becoming the best backstroker Mexico has had. Here are some of my most important competitions and achievements:

    1. 2019 World Junior Swimming Championships Budapest, Hungary

      • 18th in 100 meter backstroke
      • 22nd in 50 meter backstroke
      • 24th in 200 meter backstroke

    2. 2021 Mare Nostrum Swim Tour

      • 3rd in 50 meter backstroke and National Record: 25.85
      • 5th in 100 meter backstroke and National Record: 55.70

    3. 2021 Junior Pan-American Games Cali, Colombia

      • 1st in 100 meter backstroke and National Record: 55.38
      • 1st in 4x100 meter medley relay
      • 2nd in 200 meter backstroke
      • 2nd in 4x100 meter mixed medley relay
      • 2nd in 4x100 meter freestyle relay

    4. 2023 Central American Games San Salvador, El Salvador

      • 1st in 4x100 meter freestyle relay
      • 1st in 4x100 meter medley relay
      • 3rd in 100 meter backstroke
      • 3rd in 50 meter backstroke and National Record: 25.68

    5. 2023 Pan American Games Santiango, Chile

      • B Finalist in 100 meter backstroke
      • B Finalist in 200 meter backstroke

    6. World Swimming Championships Fukuoka, Japan

      • 35th in 100 meter backstroke
      • 38th in 50 meter backstroke
      • 17th in 4x100 meter medley relay
